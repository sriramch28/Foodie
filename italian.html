Spaghetti Carbonara :

Preparation :

1. Cook the Spaghetti Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the spaghetti. Set aside.

2. Make the Tomato Sauce Heat olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until translucent, about 3‚Äì4 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the crushed tomatoes or tomato puree. Stir to combine. Add sugar (if needed), oregano, chili flakes, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer gently for 10‚Äì15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

3. Combine Pasta and Sauce Add the cooked spaghetti directly to the sauce in the skillet. Toss well to coat the spaghetti evenly with the sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta cooking water to loosen it.

4. Serve Divide the spaghetti into serving plates or bowls. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and sprinkle grated Parmesan cheese on top. Serve immediately with a side of crusty bread or garlic bread if desired.

Tips for Perfect Spaghetti : Pasta Water: The starchy water enhances the sauce's consistency and helps it cling to the pasta.
