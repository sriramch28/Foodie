Butter Chicken :

Preparation :

1. Marinate the Chicken In a large bowl, mix the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, cumin powder, lemon juice, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and coat them well in the marinade. Cover and let it marinate for at least 1 hour (preferably overnight for better flavor).

2. Cook the Chicken Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook on medium heat until they are about 90% cooked. Set aside.

3. Prepare the Butter Chicken Sauce Heat butter and oil in the same pan. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown. Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant. Add the tomato puree and cook for 5-7 minutes until the oil separates. Add chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and garam masala. Mix well. Stir in the cashew paste and cook for another 2-3 minutes. Add water as needed to adjust the consistency of the sauce.

4. Combine Chicken with Sauce Add the cooked chicken to the sauce and let it simmer for 8-10 minutes on low heat. Stir in the fresh cream and dried fenugreek leaves (crush them between your palms before adding). Check the seasoning and adjust salt as needed.

5. Garnish and Serve Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice.

    Chicken briyani :

    Preparation :

    1. Marinate the Chicken In a large bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, lemon juice, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and coat them well with the marinade. Cover and let it marinate for at least 1 hour (preferably overnight for better flavor).

    2. Cook the Chicken Heat oil or ghee in a large pan. Add sliced onions and saut√© until golden brown. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy. Add the marinated chicken and cook on medium heat until the chicken is fully cooked and the oil separates from the masala.

    3. Layer the Biryani In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread half of the cooked rice. Layer the chicken mixture evenly on top of the rice. Add the remaining rice as the final layer. Sprinkle mint, cilantro, garam masala, and saffron milk over the rice. Optionally, add fried onions for extra flavor.

    4. Dum Cooking (Steam Cooking) Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. If the lid isn‚Äôt tight, seal the edges with dough or place a heavy weight on top. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes. This allows the flavors to blend beautifully. Turn off the heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.

    5. Serve Gently mix the layers to combine the rice and chicken. Serve hot with raita, salad, or boiled eggs on the side.
