Sushi :

Preparation :

1. Cook and Season the Rice Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear to remove excess starch. Combine rice and water in a rice cooker or pot. Cook according to the rice cooker instructions or simmer on the stovetop for about 18 minutes, then let it rest for 10 minutes. In a small saucepan, mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt. Heat gently until dissolved. Transfer the cooked rice to a large bowl. Gradually fold in the vinegar mixture while fanning the rice to cool it. Set aside to cool completely.

2. Prepare the Ingredients Slice fish and vegetables into thin, even strips. Lay out your bamboo sushi mat and cover it with plastic wrap to prevent sticking.

3. Assemble the Sushi Place a sheet of nori, shiny side down, on the bamboo mat. Wet your hands with water to prevent rice from sticking. Spread a thin, even layer of sushi rice over the nori, leaving about 1 inch at the top edge. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and salt. Arrange fish, vegetables, or other fillings horizontally across the rice, about 1 inch from the bottom edge.

4. Roll the Sushi In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and salt. Using the bamboo mat, lift the bottom edge of the nori and roll it tightly over the filling. Press gently but firmly to shape the roll as you go. Continue rolling until you reach the top edge. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and salt. Dab a little water on the top edge of the nori to seal the roll.

5. Slice and Serve Use a sharp knife to cut the sushi roll into 6‚Äì8 even pieces. Wipe the knife with a damp cloth between cuts for clean slices. Arrange the sushi on a plate and serve with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger on the side.
